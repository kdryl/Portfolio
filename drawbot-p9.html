Project Challenge

Design and build a robot that could draw shapes (squares, triangles, circles) accurately and repeatably. The main challenge was getting control theory to work on actual hardware - backlash in the belts and mechanical compliance made it much harder than the simulations suggested.

Performance Requirements

Achieve sub-millimeter positioning accuracy, complete shapes in under 10 seconds, maintain smooth motion through sharp corners, and demonstrate repeatability across multiple drawing cycles.

Technical Approach

Hardware Platform

Two-axis belt-driven gantry with DC motors, quadrature encoders, and Raspberry Pi Pico control

Control Strategy

PID controllers designed in MATLAB/Simulink with anti-windup and filtering for derivative noise

Path Planning

Python preprocessing to generate coordinate sequences from geometric shape definitions

Real-time Execution

2ms sampling period control loop with motor driver integration via L298N H-bridges

Control System Design

Started with simple bang-bang control for basic testing, then moved to PID control designed in MATLAB simulation. The system converted coordinates to motor angles based on belt ratios and kinematics. Key design choices included finding the right sampling frequency and implementing anti-windup to handle sharp corners smoothly.

Key Engineering Challenges

Backlash Compensation

Belt and gearbox play caused rounded corners instead of sharp transitions. Solved by increasing coordinate point density near corners and adjusting shape generation algorithms.

Noise Management

PID derivative term introduced control signal jitter. Implemented low-pass filtering to maintain stability while preserving response speed.

Results & Performance

Positioning Accuracy

  • Repeatability: Sub-millimeter across all shapes
  • Overshoot: Less than 4% on step responses
  • Settling Time: ~1 second target achievement
  • Path Following: Smooth curves with minimal deviation

Shape Completion

  • Squares: Excellent accuracy and sharp corners
  • Circles: Good circular geometry after polar coordinate fix
  • Triangles: Some residual distortion from backlash
  • Execution Time: 4-6 seconds per complete shape

Performance Validation

The final system successfully demonstrated reliable geometric shape plotting with quantified sub-millimeter repeatability. Control system design methodology proved effective in bridging simulation and physical implementation.

Engineering Impact

This project demonstrated the practical challenges of implementing theoretical control systems in physical hardware. The iterative design process—from simulation to hardware testing—highlighted the importance of robust control design that accounts for real-world non-idealities.

My specific contributions included electrical hardware design, MATLAB/Simulink simulation development, and embedded C++ implementation for the Raspberry Pi Pico. The project reinforced understanding of multivariable control, sensor integration, and the critical importance of systematic testing and validation.

Future Improvements

Identified opportunities included implementing feedforward control for backlash compensation, adding automatic calibration routines, and exploring MIMO control approaches for better axis coupling management. The modular design provides a strong foundation for advanced control algorithm development.
